Scenario 3：
Find the happiest city according to what people text on Twitter.

Scenario Description：

Under this scenario, we are more curious about the content of each tweet and how it can imply people's emotion. Text2emotion package is adopted to do sentiment analysis on text. Since the runtime for this package is relatively long, 2000 samples are randomly  chosen from each city to represent the overall performance. The mean score of five emotions: happy, angry,  surprise, sad and fear are collected for each city. All scores are between 0 and 1 where a low score indicates no obvious feeling in this emotion. We can then conclude people's sentiment in different areas and find out which city is the happiest one.

Figure Analysis (Happy):
This bar chart shows the happiness score of each city calculated from the tweet data. It can be seen from the figure that Melbourne contain more tweets with happy feelings. This calculation is based on natural language processing to analyze the degree of happiness in the entire sentence, and at the end it returns a score for each region to represent the degree of happiness in the current region.

Figure Analysis (Sad):
This histogram shows the sadness of each city calculated from the tweet sentiment analysis. It can be seen from the figure that Perth contains more tweets with sad feelings. Also, we can find that there is not much difference in the degree of sadness in the other 4 cities. This calculation is based on natural language processing to analyze the degree of Sadness in the entire sentence, and finally returns a score for each region to represent the degree of sadness in the current region.

Figure Analysis (Angry):
This histogram shows the degree of anger of each city calculated from the tweet sentiment analysis. It can be seen from the figure that Adelaide contain more tweets with angry feelings and Brisbane has less Angry emotion. We might conclude that based on Chart 1 and Chart 3, the Brisbane resident has happier life and low pressure. This calculation is based on natural language processing to analyze the degree of Angry in the entire sentence, and finally returns a score for each region to represent the degree of Angry in the current region.

Scenario Analysis

This Scenario shows the emotional status of people in different regions. It is not difficult to find that people living in Brisbane are less angry and sad, and more happy than others. From this we can infer that people living in Brisbane may live with less pressure. Also, the level of grief among residents in the Perth area will be significantly higher than that of other areas. There is little difference in the performance of the relationship status in other areas.
