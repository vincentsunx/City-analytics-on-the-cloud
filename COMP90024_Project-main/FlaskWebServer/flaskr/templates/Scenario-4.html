Scenario 4:
The impact of Covid-19 on the labour market.

Scenario Description:

The coronavirus affects all walks of life. During 2020, many people started to work at home. Due to the lockdown, plenty of companies are suffering from a hard time. As the company layoffs and bankruptcy increase, we want to investigate how the labour market changed before and after the pandemic. In this section, we are using labour market summary data collected at the end of 2018 and 2020 to make the comparison.

Chart Analysis:                                 

The diagram shows the unemployment rate of 2018 and 2020. Because of COVID 19 the unemployment rate increased a lot in 2020. Based on the situation in Australia, it can be inferred that Australia may be due to the impact of COVID-19 on the entire market, which has caused the unemployment rate to drop a lot in 2020.

Chart Analysis:                                 

Compare to year 2018, the youth employment rate increases at 2020. Companies prefer to use youth labour, it provides stable work.The interesting point is that although the overall unemployment rate has increased in 2020, the unemployment rate of young people has decreased, which means that more young people have found jobs and at meanwhile more employees have left their jobs. Therefore,we could infer that companies are more willing to recruit young people under the current circumstances.

Figure Title:  Vaccine complete rate in 5 regions

Chart Analysis:
The graph shows that vaccine complete proporation in 5 cities. According to this graph, we can know that Brisbane's vaccination rate is the lowest, and Perth's vaccination rate is the highest. It can be inferred that people living in Perth are more willing and acceptance of vaccines is relatively high.

Chart Analysis:

It shows the relation between 2020 unemployment rate and tweet happiness score in 5 major cities. Comparing the happy status sentiment with the unemployment rate in each city, we discovered that Sydney has the highest unemployment rate changed, and the second lower happiness score which is almost the same as Perth. Melbourne as a culture center of Australia has an outstanding happiness sentiment which verifies the fact that Melbourne is one of the most livable cities. Overall, the larger cities with a high development level tend to have a greater change of the employment rate. In contrast, Perth has both the lowest employment rate and the lowest happiness sentiment, because Perth itself has the highest unemployment rate, although it has not changed much before and after the epidemic, it is the city with the lowest happiness index.

Scenario
Analysis:

In conclusion, the pandemic affects the unemployment rate in some extent, but it is not the only cause. Although the Covid-19 situation is most serious in VIC, Greater Sydney has the largest unemployment rate change before and after the pandemic. It also gains a low score in sentiment analysis of ‘happiness’.
 The employment rate for young people increases at the end of 2020(except Perth) as the overall unemployment rate increases. Perth is the least happy city among all. Though it has the lowest unemployment rate change, the original unemployment rate is higher than most of the cities. So, the pandemic didn’t affect the unemployment rate much, but the government needs to further study the long-term labor market to decrease the unemployment rate and we may expect an improvement in ‘happiness’ score as well.
